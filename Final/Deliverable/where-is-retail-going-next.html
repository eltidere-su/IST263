Where is Retail Going Next?

It is a common myth that brick and mortar retail is dying. There have been a lot of physical retail locations that have closed in the past couple of years. This is due to the flourishing of ecommerce. Most people think that consumers do not shop in stores anymore becasue people prefer to shop from the convenience of their own home.

There are three main things that today's customers are looking for out of a retail expereince; convenience, personalization, and customization. As companies are adapting to the transformation in retail, each company has adopted different strategies to thrive in the new retail world. Each company has a differnt way of catering to some or all of these demands. The companies that excel above the rest are the companies that fulfill all of the demands of today's consumer.

Brick and mortar retail is not ending but the way physical stores are being used is evolving. Stores are now being used to support the customer journey. Before stores were the only place the transaction would take please. Now the store might not be where the transaction occurs, instead it might be the place that customers try on articles of clothing or get a feel for the brand.

The sucess of brands depends on their ability to respond to the changing retail environment. Many companies have resorted to using the latest technologies to enhance the in-store expereince for their customers.

With the technology revolution in full swing, technology has been used in many different ways recetly. Retailers are taking these technologies and implementing them into their stores to provide a better expereince than their competitors.

Some of these technologies include RFID, beacons, and geolocation. Explore the website to discover specifically how these technologies work and which companies have successfully implemented them to rise above their competition.
